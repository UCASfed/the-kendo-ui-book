Chapter 1. Why Kendo UI

1.1 Who Should Read This Book

Unlike traditional encyclopedias, Wikipedia can be edited by any reader. Except for particularly sensitive and/or vandalism-prone pages that are "protected" to some degree,[23] Wikipedia visitors can edit articles even if they do not have a Wikipedia account. Certain language editions offer a modified version of this policy; for example, only registered users may create a new article in the English edition.[24] A frequently vandalized article can be semi-protected, meaning that only established, verified users are able to make edits to that article.[25] A particularly contentious article may be locked so that only administrators are able to make changes.[26] An article is not considered to be owned by its creator or any other editor and is not vetted by any recognized authority.[27] Rather, editors are encouraged to discuss differing options regarding the content and structure of articles in order to come to agreement.
